Nikolai Kapustin: Piano Sonata No. 1 "Sonata Fantasia" Op. 39 - IV. Allegro Molto (1984)

Kapustin uses traditional Sonata-allegro form as a structure with which to build on with his idiosyncratic style. A brief breakdown:

Exposition
  • 0:00 - 0:35: Introduction
  • 0:36 - 1:25: Primary theme
  • 1:26 - 1:47: Transition
  • 1:48 - 2:44 : Secondary theme (in relative major key)
  • 2:45 - 3:24 : Closing material (Codetta)

Development
  • 3:25 - 4:20

Recapitulation
  • 4:21 - 4:52: Primary theme (shorter version)
  • 4:53 - 5:38: Secondary theme (in parallel major key)
  • 5:39 - 6:20 : Closing material (Codetta)
  • 6:21 - 6:54 : Coda

Read more >


The sonata begins with a shirt but energetic introduction in d minor. There is a "call-and-response" thing going on here, where the bass poses a question, then the treble clef answers with a few chords. A fast run of 16th notes leads to the next question and answer. Kapustin uses lots of extended chords such as major and minor 9ths and 11ths to build on top of the deep bass notes. A few variations of this lead to a mini-ending which arpeggiates up the keyboard and rings out
